Basic Lasagna Recipe

When it comes to comfort food, there's just nothing better than pasta and cheese. Whether you prefer a meat lasagna or a vegetarian noodle dish, we've gathered up the best lasagna recipes that make the most of this classic combination. Get ready to take this dinner standby from good to great with these delicious, creative lasagna recipes that can be served at family meals, for entertaining dinner guests, at potluck parties and for holiday celebrations

Ingrediets

For Sauce

  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound ground beef chuck (chuck or round)
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

For Lasagna

  • 8 ounces lasagna noodles
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 16 ounces low-fat ricotta cheese
  • 11/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 16 ounces mozzarella cheese (fresh, whole milk, sliced)

Steps to Make it

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Start the sauce about 30 minutes in advance. In a large deep saucepan over medium heat, saut√© the onion and garlic in 3 tablespoons of olive oil until onions are soft.
  3. Add the ground beef and brown, stirring frequently.
  4. Add the tomato paste and cook, stirring until bubbling, about 3 minutes. Add water, tomatoes, salt, basil, cayenne pepper, and bay leaf. Stir well.
  5. Reduce heat to low and simmer slowly, uncovered, for 15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

Assemble the lasagna and Bake

  1. Bring a stockpot full of water to a rolling boil and season with salt. Add the lasagna noodles and cook as the package directs, stirring frequently to prevent sticking. Drain and toss gently with 1 tablespoon of olive oil.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350 F and grab a large 3-quart rectangular baking dish.
  3. Spoon 4 to 6 tablespoons of the sauce or just enough to cover the bottom of the baking dish.
  4. Top with a single layer of noodles. Spoon about 1/3 of the remaining sauce over the noodles. Spoon about 1/2 of the ricotta cheese evenly over the layer of sauce, then sprinkle a few tablespoons of Parmesan cheese over that. Add about 1/3 of the mozzarella cheese.
  5. Repeat with another 1/3 of noodles, another 1/3 of the sauce, the remaining ricotta, another 1/3 of the mozzarella and a few tablespoons of Parmesan cheese.
  6. Finish with the last layer of noodles, the remaining sauce, Parmesan, and mozzarella cheese.
  7. Bake the lasagna, uncovered, for 45 minutes. If the top is browning too fast or the noodles are burning, cover loosely with aluminum foil.
  8. Remove from oven and let cool 10 minutes before slicing into squares and serving.
